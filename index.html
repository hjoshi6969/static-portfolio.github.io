About Me version 19 years old
close
My name is Himanshu Joshi, and I am a web developer. I was born in a small town in Bharat (India). My childhood was great until I turned nine years old, as that was the time I suffered from a brain disease. Consequently, I had to take medicines for six years, turning me into a cute but overweight child.

When I turned ten, my father sadly passed away in a tragic incident, completely turning my life upside down. Upon joining high school, I found myself overweight and struggling academically, ranking second to last in the entire school.

In the 10th standard, I joined the gym, started boxing and dancing, and things began to improve. I got in shape and even had my first girlfriend. However, despite these positive changes, I realized I was still struggling academically when I entered the 11th standard.

Then, a challenging situation arose — my girlfriend and I broke up due to personal confusion. This triggered a familiar cycle; I gained weight again, not due to medication but fast food. I felt down, and to make matters worse, I nearly failed the first term of my 12th standard with only 10 days left for the final exams. I hadn't studied at all, and the idea of repeating the class crossed my mind.

In that dramatic situation, someone shared something about me that changed my mindset. I started thinking rationally and realized I needed to score 80/100 to be eligible to apply to a college in Canada (a dream destination for my studies). With just seven days left, I dedicated myself to preparing for the finals and managed to score 79/100. Fortunately, I got accepted into the RRC, where I am currently scoring A+ in all my subjects and making positive decisions in my life.

However, I must admit that I still carry a few extra pounds ☹️.
